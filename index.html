Regent Enterprise Management

In 2014, I transferred into full-time Interaction Design when I moved to Regent Education, a software company in Frederick, MD. Their main product at the time was a suite of software used by institutions such as colleges to calculate, award and manage financial aid for students. Their unique selling point was being the only suite that allowed for non-traditional school terms, meaning they could manage everything from a standard, 4-year degree to one taken part-time over ten years.

Amongst other things, I conducted an extensive heuristic review of Regent's then-current software suite, Regent Enterprise Management 8. Other highlights included the creation of a Student Financial Calculator, one of the few products intended to be publicly facing.

Typically, though, I worked on smaller problems, such as dealing with very large tables on small screens, or dropdowns that a customer had populated with 300+ items. Alongside those I tackled larger redesign and optimisation efforts, which resulted in these requirements for a commonly-used and complex screen.
